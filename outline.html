Back to Christ

Exposing the Judaizing Error — Law, Grace & the Sufficiency of Jesus

Central Question: Is Christ enough? Or do we need Christ plus something else?

I. The Problem Emerges

  • Judaizers: insiders teaching Christ + Torah for salvation
  • Paul called it "a different gospel" (Gal. 1:6-7)
  • Adding to Christ = Christ's work wasn't enough
Galatians 1:8 — Even an angel preaching another gospel is cursed

II. The Jerusalem Council (Acts 15)

  • Peter: God gave Spirit to uncircumcised Gentiles
  • Law = "yoke neither we nor fathers could bear" (v.10)
  • Verdict: "We gave no such commandment" (v.24)

III. Paul Confronts Peter (Gal. 2)

  • Peter withdrew from Gentiles — actions preached false gospel
  • "Not acting in line with the truth of the gospel" (v.14)
Galatians 2:21 — "If righteousness comes through law, Christ died for nothing!"

IV. The Purpose of the Law

  • Added because of transgressions (Gal. 3:19)
  • Guardian/tutor until Christ came (Gal. 3:24)
  • Reveals sin but cannot remedy it (Rom. 3:20)
  • Holy and good, but not source of life (Gal. 3:21)
Galatians 3:24-25 — Law was guardian; now faith has come, no longer under guardian

V. Shadow and Substance

  • OT ceremonies = shadows; reality = Christ (Col. 2:17)
  • Repeated sacrifices proved inadequacy (Heb. 10:1-4)
  • Christ's ONE sacrifice = perfect forever (Heb. 10:14)
Colossians 2:17 — "These are a shadow... the reality is found in Christ"

VI. The New Covenant (Jer. 31, Ezek. 36)

  • "Not like" the old covenant (Jer. 31:32)
  • Law written on hearts, not stone
  • Direct knowledge of God — "all will know me"
  • Complete forgiveness — sins remembered no more
  • Old covenant = obsolete (Heb. 8:13)

VII. Freedom in Christ

  • "For freedom Christ set us free" (Gal. 5:1)
  • Freedom to love and serve, not license (Gal. 5:13-14)
  • Under grace, sin has no dominion (Rom. 6:14)
  • Spirit produces what law demanded (Gal. 5:22-23)
Galatians 5:1 — "Stand firm, do not be burdened again by slavery"

VIII. Resting in Christ Alone

  • "It is finished" (tetelestai) = paid in full (John 19:30)
  • Christ SAT DOWN — work complete (Heb. 1:3)
  • No condemnation in Christ (Rom. 8:1)
  • Obedience flows FROM acceptance, not toward it
Modern Judaizers: Any "Christ plus..." for salvation

Memory Verses:

Gal. 2:21 — "If righteousness through law, Christ died for nothing"

Gal. 5:1 — "For freedom Christ has set us free"

Col. 2:17 — "Shadow... reality is found in Christ"

Heb. 10:14 — "By one sacrifice, perfect forever"

Reflection Questions:

  • Where am I tempted to add to Christ's finished work?
  • What "yokes" have I placed on myself or others?
  • Am I living FROM God's approval or FOR it?
